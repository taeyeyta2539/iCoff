Goats' milk contains a higher level of vitamin A than cows' milk due to the fact that goats convert all carotenes into vitamin A, which gives the milk a whitish color. Goats' milk supplies adequate amounts of vitamin A and niacin, and excesses of thiamin, riboflavin, and pantothenate for human infants (Table 6). Both goats' and cows' milk are equally deficient in vitamin C and D; therefore, these vitamins must be supplemented with other food sources. Goats' milk contains only 20% as much folic acid as cows' milk.
